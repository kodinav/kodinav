# Search Console Check-in Guide

Come back to this ~4–6 weeks after launch (so around late August 2026), then
roughly monthly. The point is to let Google gather real data on your 26 posts,
then use that data to decide what to do next instead of guessing.

Site launched: July 2026. Sitemap submitted: yes. 26 blog posts live.

---

## 1. Coverage — is Google actually indexing the site?

Search Console → **Pages** (under Indexing).

- Look at **"Indexed" vs "Not indexed"** counts.
- Goal: most of your ~54 URLs indexed. Some "Crawled - currently not indexed" is
  normal for a new site; it usually resolves with time.
- If lots are "Discovered - currently not indexed" after 6+ weeks, the site needs
  more authority (backlinks) — not more pages.

**Action:** For 3–4 of your best commercial posts (cost, hiring, custom-vs-wordpress),
paste the URL into the top search bar → **Request indexing** to nudge Google.

---

## 2. Performance — what are you starting to rank for?

Search Console → **Performance → Search results**. Set date range to **last 28 days**.

Look at these tabs:

- **Queries** — the actual searches showing your site. Even at position 40+, this
  tells you which keywords Google associates with you. Gold for deciding what to
  write next.
- **Pages** — which of your pages get the most impressions. Your early winners.
- **Position** — anything landing on page 2 (positions 11–20) is a near-miss worth
  strengthening.

**What the numbers mean:**
- *Impressions* = how often you appeared in search (even far down). Rising = good.
- *Clicks* = people who actually visited from search.
- *Average position* = where you rank. Lower is better. New sites often start at
  30–90 and climb over months.

---

## 3. Decide what to do next (this is the whole point)

Use the data above, in this order:

1. **A post is getting impressions but sits at position 11–20 (page 2).**
   → Expand and improve THAT post. Add depth, answer related questions, add internal
   links to it from other posts. Pushing a page-2 post onto page 1 beats writing a
   brand-new post from scratch.

2. **A topic/keyword shows up in Queries that you don't have a post for.**
   → Write that post. Google is telling you what it already half-associates with you.

3. **A whole topic cluster is getting traction** (e.g. the pricing/hiring posts).
   → Write 2–3 more in that cluster. Double down on proven ground.

4. **Nothing is moving yet after 6 weeks.**
   → Do NOT write more posts. The problem is authority, not content. Focus on:
   Google Business Profile + reviews, and backlinks (directories, client footer
   credit links, partnerships).

---

## 4. Cadence going forward

- Publish **2–4 posts per month**, chosen from the data above, not in bulk.
- 24 topics remain in `blog-strategy.md` for when the data points to them.
- A steadily-updated blog signals an active business to Google. A one-time dump
  does not.

---

## The higher-leverage work (do these regardless of blog data)

1. **Google Business Profile** live + **3–5 real client reviews** (Lighthouse,
   Triplipi, Trinket). Biggest local-ranking lever there is.
2. **Backlinks:** get listed in a few developer/business directories; ask clients
   whose sites you built to add a small "Built by Kodinav" credit link.
3. **Calendly + Meta Pixel** when ready (send IDs to wire in).
