# First Client Playbook

Goal: one paying project (₹75,000+) with zero ad spend, to fund the ads budget.
Everything here is ready to copy, personalise the [brackets], and send.

The order below is probability order. Warm beats cold, always.

---

## 1. Past clients — referral + testimonial (send all 6 today)

Lighthouse Classes, Achiever's Hive, Triplipi, Trinket, Kosmo Dental, Flaming.
These are the highest-probability messages you will ever send. WhatsApp, casual:

> Hi [Name], Abhinav here. Hope [the platform / the store / the site] is
> running well.
>
> Quick thing: I've set up my studio properly now — kodinav.com — and your
> project is one of the case studies on it. Two small favours if you have a
> minute:
>
> 1. If you know anyone who needs a website or an app built, an intro would
> mean a lot. I'll take good care of them.
> 2. Could you send me two honest lines about how it was working with me?
> Real client words go on the site, nothing formal needed.
>
> Either way, if you ever need changes or something breaks, you know where
> I am.

Why it works: it is a status update, not a pitch. The favour is small and
specific. The last line reminds them you are their safety net, which is worth
referrals on its own.

**Bonus ask (send later, separately, only to the friendliest ones):** a small
"Built by Kodinav" link in their site footer. It is a real SEO backlink.

## 3. Cold outreach — the audit wedge (10 per day)

The routine that actually produces a client in 2 to 5 weeks:

1. Google Maps: search "coaching institute [Noida/your area]", "dental clinic
   [area]", "hotel [area]". Open their websites ON YOUR PHONE.
2. Pick ones that are slow, broken on mobile, or ancient. Run the site
   through PageSpeed Insights (free) and note ONE specific number.
3. Find the owner's WhatsApp/phone (usually on the site or GMB listing).
4. Send this, personalised with the real finding:

**Coaching institute version:**

> Hi [Name], I'm Abhinav, a software engineer in Delhi NCR. I came across
> [Institute]'s website today. It takes [9] seconds to open on a phone,
> and most parents searching for coaching leave before that. I build fast
> websites for coaching institutes (recent work: kodinav.com/work).
> Happy to send you a short, free breakdown of what I found. Takes me ten
> minutes, no obligation. Want me to?

**Clinic version:**

> Hi Dr. [Name], I'm Abhinav, a software engineer in Delhi NCR. I looked at
> [Clinic]'s website today. It doesn't show properly on a phone, which is
> where nearly all patients search. I recently built the website for a
> dental clinic (kodinav.com/work/kosmo-dental-clinic). Happy to send a
> short free note on what I'd fix, no obligation. Want me to?

**Rules that make this work instead of being spam:**
- The [finding] must be TRUE and SPECIFIC. That one number is the whole
  difference between an engineer and a spammer.
- 10 a day, personalised, beats 100 generic. Thirty minutes daily.
- The ask is tiny ("want the breakdown?"), never "buy a website".
- When they say yes: send 5 honest bullet points same day, end with
  "If you want this fixed properly, projects start at ₹75,000. Happy to
  talk it through on a quick call."

**Follow-up (once, after 3 days, then stop):**

> Hi [Name], floating this back up in case it got buried. If it's not a
> priority right now, no problem at all. The offer stands whenever useful.

## 4. The weekly rhythm

- **Today:** 6 past-client messages + the announcement post.
- **Daily (30–45 min):** 10 cold audit messages, tracked in a simple sheet
  (name, sent date, reply, next step).
- **Within 1 hour:** answer every reply. Speed is the highest-converting
  thing you control.
- **Every yes:** breakdown same day → call → fixed written quote within 48h.

Realistic maths: 6 warm asks + ~50 cold messages/week → 5–10 replies →
2–4 real conversations → **first close typically in week 2–5.** One close
funds the entire ad experiment.

## 5. What NOT to do

- Don't discount out of desperation. ₹75,000 with a written scope closes
  better than ₹40,000 with nervous energy. Cheap clients are the hardest.
- Don't build spec work for free to "prove yourself". The portfolio proves it.
- Don't wait for the perfect moment to post the announcement. Post it.
